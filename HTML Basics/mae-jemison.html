Mae Jemison

"The best way to make dreams come true is to wake up."

Mae Carol Jemison was born on October 17, 1956. She is an engineer, physician and an astronaut. On September 12, 1992, she became the first African-American woman to travel in space when she went into orbit aboard the Space Shuttle Endeavour. She is also a dancer and an actress, appearing in an epsidoe of Star Trek: Next Generation.

Interesting Facts

  1. Jemison began dancing at the age of 11
  2. She entered Standford University at the age of 16
  3. She served in the Peace Corps from 1983 to 1985
  4. In 1987, she was one of fifteen candidates chosen out of roughly 2,000 applicants to NASA
